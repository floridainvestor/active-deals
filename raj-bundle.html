Negotiation Sequence

How to Present This

1. Lead with the bundle value. "$3,250/mo in passive income from one buyer, secured by three properties. $460K in interest over 10 years. $1.24M total received. Zero management." Let him sit with those numbers.

2. Acknowledge his problem. "These are great houses, but they're the biggest in their neighborhoods. That makes them hard to move on the open market. I have a use case that makes them work — and I'm willing to take both at once."

3. Present the terms as a package. Don't negotiate one property at a time — that lets him optimize each individually. Present the bundle: "Here's what I need to make the math work on both." The flat rate on Forest Hills is offset by the graduated rate on Skycrest. Bundle = compromise.

4. Frame the extension option as insurance FOR HIM. "If the refi market is ugly in Year 10, you don't want me forced into a fire sale. The extension option means you keep earning income and I don't have to rush. It protects your principal."

5. Close with the simplicity pitch. "One closing, one buyer, one relationship. I wire you $3,250 on the first of every month. You never get a tenant call, a repair bill, or an insurance claim. That's the deal."
